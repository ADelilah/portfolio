What do biologists do

Research

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis is a lethal disease. There are no treatments increasing patient's life significantly. The Cell Technologies Group of IPAC RAS (I'll call just "lab") is studying a specific gene known as one of the ALS causes: FUS. For that they use two lines of mice:

  • FUS - transgenic mice with FUS gene, i.e. ill mice.
  • WT - wild type - normal healthy mice.

As ALS is a neurodegenerative disease, is affects motor features. Observing motor features we can draw certain conclusions about disease states and effectiveness of possible treatments. (Hope you'll excuse my possible biological inaccuracy :)

Observations

Back in time, to measure motor features biologists used to dip a mouse into ink, make it walk on a piece of paper and then measure the footprints.

Now they have a device for it. I have no idea why Catwalk XT is called so, while both cats and fashion models are too big to put them in. But imagine it as a glass corrigor with a camera under the floor. Camera captures mouse footprints (in some complicated way you may not care about). Then software coming with Catwalk is calculating some statistics - a row of numbers per animal.

It's a hard life

To produce a row of data we need 4 valid (with certain dispersion) runs per animal. While animal sometimes prefer to pee or sit in a corner shivering instead of running. While biologists can't pee during the experement - it happens on an aseptic floor. On the top of it they used to do ~ 600 clicks per animal in Catwalk soft (footprint measurements) to calculate statistics. But we changed it.

I supposed to find out if it possible to use machine learning on Catwalk data, then what features are important, and finally - if the treatment works.
